Modernization is rarely a clean replacement project. Existing platforms carry revenue, customer history, operational knowledge, and hidden dependencies. The plan must protect that value while creating room to change.

## Define the business reason

Clarify whether modernization is intended to improve speed, reliability, customer experience, operating cost, data access, or the ability to launch new products. Architecture choices should follow that priority.

## Create transition boundaries

Identify capabilities that can be separated and improved without destabilizing the whole platform. Incremental boundaries make progress visible and reduce migration risk.

## Measure capability, not completion

Track improvements in release speed, availability, conversion, support load, and team productivity. A modern stack is only valuable when the business can do something better because of it.
